The Amount

The amount of crashes happening per year is quite significant.
Keep in mind what we see are only what's reported.
The only thing that was able to reduce the amount of accidents was the pandemic when most people are off the roads.
There is clearly not enough being done to improve road safety if they are consistently reporting the same amount of crashes each year.

The Trend

Over these six years, we can see that the afternoon to evening hours have the most crashes.
However, the most amount of people using the roads is during the morning and afternoon commutes.
It can be inferred that we don't see as big of a spike in the morning because people are more likely to be alert.

The Causes

An OVERWHELMING majority of crashes happen due to driver error.
One of the biggest causes is "following too closely" which is tailgating!
Something so simple to correct should not be this prevalent in this data, but here it is.
On the bright side, driver error can be corrected. It's just that there isn't enough being done.

The Impact

What do all these crashes amount to?
Thousands of injuries and thousands of totaled cars.
